Case Pattern: Environmental / Mycotoxin

Fine in some buildings. Sick in others. The pattern points to something specific.

About a third of my chronic inflammation cases come in the same way. Client has tried everything — antihistamines, gut work, nervous system regulation, elimination diet. Some things help for a week. Then the symptoms come back. Usually in the same places. Usually at the same times.

Before the next protocol change, I ask them to track a map. Which rooms. Which buildings. Which days. Within two weeks the pattern is almost always obvious. Fine at work. Sick at home. Or fine in the new office. Sick in the car. Or fine on vacation. Back to square one in the bedroom.

This is almost always a mycotoxin load pattern. Not mold generally — a specific mycotoxin, from a specific building material, producing a specific symptom cluster. Ochratoxin from water-damaged drywall looks different from aflatoxin from contaminated food. Gliotoxin looks different from trichothecenes. Eight mycotoxins. Eight symptom profiles. Broad-spectrum treatment keeps missing because the target is specific.
